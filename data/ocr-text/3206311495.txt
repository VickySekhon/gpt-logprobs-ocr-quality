| DARING ROBB RY AT A CHURCH. On Monday morning, at an early hour, it was dis- covered that a most daring robbery had been committed in the vestry-room of the parish church of St. Giles. without, Cripplegate, by some expert thieves. Late in the evening before (Sunday), the potman of Mr. Wiltshire, whose establishment adjoins the yard of the church at the back, nearly contiguous to the window of the vestry-room, heard something like a tapping at a pane of glass, and a sudden rustling as he passed his master's back door, before the hour of clos. ing. Not taking any farther notice of this circum. stance, however, he made fast the back entrance, and with the family retired to rest. On Monday morning when he took down the back shutters, he found the vestry window in the churchyard broken, and the let. tice-work torn from its place.
