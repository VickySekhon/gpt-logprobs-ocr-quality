On Saturday night, the 20th of October, the defendant went to the prosecutrix's door and knocked, and on it being opened to him, he hamediately gave her a violent blow in the face and ran away. From this blow she suffered considerable pain. The prisoner was subsequently arrested, and charged with the offence.-The jury found the prisoner " Gnilty."-The prosecutrix stated that since this case had been pending she had been threatened by the witnesses who had been called for the prisoner with violence if she dared to give evidence against him.-Mr. Serjeant Cox sentenced the prisoner to be kept to hard labour for nine months, and after that to find two sureties in 101. each, and to enter into his own recognisances in the sum of 201., to keep the peace for twelve months.-The witnesses alluded to were then called forward, and one in particular was cautioned as to his conduct, and the learned serjeait further advised the prosecutrix to take out a summons against him. A FORTUNATE PRISONER. - Mary Cray, whose real name is said to be Malary Ann Cole, surrendered in discharge of her bail, to answer an indictment charging her with having stolen a case of needles, value Is., the property of William Whiteley. -The evidence tended to show that the accused went into one of the shops of Mr. Whiteley, at Westbournegrove, on the afternoon of November 2, and made a purchase; that an assistant saw her take a packet of needles; and that she ultimately confessed that she bad done so, said it was the first time she bad done anything of the kind, and offered to pay for the article,-Mr. Williams addressed the jury for the prisoner, and called three witnesses, who gave her a good character.-The jury, to the surprise of most of the persons in court. returned a verdict of "Not guilty."
