BABY STARVING.-On Tuesday evening Mr. William Carter held an inquiry at the Camberwell workhouse respecting the death of Ellen Hayley, aged 10 weeks. -Mary Marks 'stated that she was a nurse in the female receiving ward ot Vamberwell workhouse, and that on Thursday week a female with an infant child in her arms entered the ward. She stated that her name was Sarah Hayley, and that the infant was her daughter, and was named Fllen. Witness had the mother and child placed in a bed. The mother stated that she was a single woman, and that she had been employed asadomestic servant. The child lookedvery emaciated, and witness called Dr. Ring, the resident medical officer, to see it. That gentleman said. " The baby is in a dying condition, and all it wants is proper diet." He then ordered food, which was ndministered to the infant. It gradually sank, and expired on Thursday. Shortly after the death of the child the mother left the workhouse, but she was immediately arrested by the police. -Detective-serjeaut Stevens deposed that he was sent for by the Rev.
