SERIOUS INJURY TO A CONSTABLE.

THOMAS BARNBY, also known as Thomas Marks, and stated to be a convict at
large on licence, was charged, before Mr. Bushby, at the Worship-street
Police-court, with having been concerned with others not in custody in
stealing from the person of Samuel Hawley a silver watch, and further
with violently assaulting him. There was a second charge against the
prisoner of having, in concert with others, violently assaulted
Police-constable Heath, 479 G, and occasioned him bodily harm. At a
quarter to ten o'clock on the night of the 2nd inst. the prosecutor, Mr.
Hawley, who resides in King Henry-road, Ball's Pond, was at the tramway
terminus, Finsbury-pavement, where he was hustled, and he then
discovered that his watch was missing. He accused a man, who he could
not be certain was the prisoner, and was assaulted, thrown down, and
kicked. Police-constable 479 G, who was on duty near the spot, rendered
assistance, and was severely maltreated, being knocked down and kicked.
He was badly injured about the body and head, lost several teeth, and
received a fracture of the jaw. He is not yet in a condition to give
evidence. The prisoner escaped at the time, but was apprehended several
days after- wards by Detective-sergeant Merroney, G Division. The
prisoner was remanded by Mr. Bushby till such time as the constable can
give evidence.

WILLIAM HEGARTY and Robert Vallance, both labourers, were charged with
being concerned in violently assaulting Police-constable 517, T
Division. It appeared that on Tuesday afternoon the constable, who was
off duty in plain clothes, was passing through Imperial-road, Fulham,
when he saw the prisoners stripped, fighting. He went up to separate
them, and was knocked down by Vallance. While down he was kicked by
Hegarty. He rose and took Hegarty into custody, but that prisoner was
rescued by a number of roughs. The prisoners went to the bank of the
river and renewed the fight. Witness followed and arrested Hegarty, but
was again knocked down and thrown over on to the foreshore. Two other
constables came to his assistance and the prisoners were secured. The
prisoner Hegarty, who stated that he had knocked off work on account of
the rain, alleged that he was not aware that 517 was a constable. The
constable said he was known to Hegarty. Mr. Curtis-Bennett sentenced
Hegarty to six weeks and Vallance to one month's imprisonment.
