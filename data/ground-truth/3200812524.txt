BOYS BURYING STOLEN MONEY.

AT the Southend Petty Sessions, on Saturday, Arthur Wright, fourteen,
and Basil Wright, twelve, two brothers, were charged with stealing £180
gold from the house of John Truss, a retired beer-house keeper.
Prosecutor, who lives in a cottage with a tarpaulin roof, said the money
was the proceeds of the sale of his beer-house. He gave it to his
daughter to keep safely, and she hid it is a canvas bag between the
mattress and the bedding of her own bed. Police-constable Prime said he
was at the Southend Railway-station the morning after the robbery, and
although he knew nothing of the theft he followed the boys as they
looked suspicious. They made numerous purchases, and he tracked there
home at South Benfleet, with the result that the boys admitted they had
broken into prosecutor’s house and stolen the money. The younger
prisoner revealed where the cash had been hidden, and going with the
constable dug up from underneath a hedge £137 in sovereigns. The lads
got to know where the money was through being playmates with
prosecutor’s young son. £40 of the money is still missing. The case was
remanded.
