TRIALS AT THE ASSIZES.

MURDEROUS ATTACK BY A LUNATIC. —William Edward Wright, teacher, of Hull,
was charged at York Assizes, on Tuesday, with attempting to murder his
wife last June. Mrs. Wright was awoke by her husband attacking her with
knife. She received several dangerous wounds. There was no motive for
the attack, and it was proved that the prisoner had been subject to
delusions. The jury found Wright insane, and, he was ordered to be
detained during Her Majesty's pleasure.

A WOMAN SENTENCED TO DEATH. —Sarah Ann Albery was sentenced to death at
Notts Assizes on Thursday for the murder of her illegitimate child by
drowning it in the river Trent. The jury made a strong recommendation to
mercy.

SOLICITOR SENTENCED. —At Leeds Assizes, on Thursday, Edwin T. Barker,
fifty, a Leeds solicitor, who pleaded guilty to embezzling money
belonging to clients, was sentenced to three years' penal servitude.
Prisoner had speculated in land and buildings, and had done some
betting. His total defalcations amount to over £20,000.

A LEEDS BREACH OF PROMISE. —Miss Gledhill, daughter of a draper at
Golcar, near Huddersfield, was awarded £50 and costs, at Leeds Assizes,
on Friday, against Mr. W. Jilling, manufacturers, for breach of promise
of marriage and seduction.

AN OFFER OF MARRIAGE IN COURT. —Charles Edward Allfrey, twenty-nine,
police-constable, was indicted at Sussex Assizes for improper conduct
with a girl named Edith Hutson, under the age of sixteen years, at
Selmeston, near Lowes. The prisoner was police-constable at the village,
and lodged with the girl's parents. He pleaded "Guilty," and offered to
marry the prosecutrix, but the parents declined to give their consent.
Baron Pollock imposed a sentence of four months' hard labour.
