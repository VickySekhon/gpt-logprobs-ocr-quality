MONETARY REVIEW.

CITY, SATURDAY AFTERNOON. —The feature of the week was Thursday's
advance in the Bank rate—from 3 to 4 per cent. Under the influence of
realizations caused by this increase, the Stock markets were generally
dull. Home railways were especially flat, being affected by the
continuance of the dock strike, and Brighton Deferred. Great Northern A,
and Sheffield Deferred fell from 1 to 13/4 per cent. The rest of the
markets were dull, but there was no decline. Yesterday a very moderate
business was done, and prices, on the whole, were dull. Home railways,
apart from Brighton Deferred, were all lower at the last, the decline
ranging from ¼ to 6/8 per cent. In Foreign Bonds there was no feature
beyond a little improvement in Peruvian issues. American railways, after
being weak, left off stronger on better prices from New York, and Grand
Trunk stocks were a shade higher. To-day business was extremely quiet,
the attendance of members being small. The markets, however, were firm
for the speculative descriptions, but Consols gave way 1/8 per cent. to
97 3/8 to 97 ¾ for both money and the account. In Home railways,
Brighton Deferred, North British deferred, and, North-Eastern rose ½.
South-Eastern deferred 1/8. Among Foreign bonds, Egyptian Domain
advanced ¼, ditto Daira, Italian Five per cents. ¼. In American and
Canadian railways Louisville improved 7/8.

During the week ending Wednesday night there was an efflux of 315,000l.
in gold from the Bank of England. The sum of 50,000l. in sovereigns was
withdrawn from the Bank for Egypt to-day.
