CUTTINGS FROM THE COMIC JOURNALS

(From Punch.)

GROUND GAME. —Lawn Tennis.

MIDSUMMER APPEALS. —Appeals for ice, claret-cup, pic-nics,
garden-parties, and light summer dresses.

GROUND GAME, &c.!!—Squire (rather perplexed): Hello, Pat! Where did you
get the hare? –Pat: Shure Surr, the cr'atur' was wandering' about, an' I
thought I'd take't to the "Wanes"! –Squire: But did the keeper see you?
–Pat: Bliss yer honour, I've been lookin' for him iver since I caught
him!!

(From Funny Folks.)

WHAT France says to the Jesuit. —"Go father, and fare worse."

THE PORTE TO THE POWERS.

  Of territory you are all so greedy,

  You make me cede a lot because I'm seed-y!

A NEW DEPARTURE — Actors have always been good at "gags," but they have
hitherto been confined to the stage. Gagging now extends to audiences.

(From ##).

A GOOD Place for Breeding Rabbits—Co(l)ney Hatch!

LUBBERLY DELUSIONS. –That the new steel corvettes in our navy are
provided with steel-yards, and that they use these steel-yards to weigh
their anchors with.

MIDSUMMER MADNESS. –Wagmore: Now, girls, what queen of antiquity
surpassed even Titania in her tondness for donkeys? –You give it up?
–Why, Dido, of course, for she fell in love with any ass. —Enas, don't
you see.

(From Judy.)

NOTICE OF MOTION. —The railway whistle.

FOOD FOR THE MINED. —Candles.

A MEAN (MIEN) ADVANTAGE. —Superiority in appearance.

QUITE RIGHT, DEAR. —Girl (looking at herself in pretty bathing-dress):
It does seem a dreadful pity to wet it.

LARK ISHNESS. –First Young Lady: I hate this horrid walking; we look
just like the animals coming out of the ark. –Second ditto: Ark-adian
bliss compared to this, Maude; they had a gentleman to see them out.
Noah would be decidedly preferable to Miss M'Stinger.

(From Moonshine.)

"DISTRESSED UNIONS." –Bad marriages.

WHAT the Theatrical Manager said when he found the dress-circle,
second-circle, and upper-circle, all empty. –"Oh, ye tiers!"

HISTORY LESSON. —School Teacher: Now, then! Next boy, George 111. died
in 1820, who came after him? –Scholar: Dunne, sir. –School Teacher: Next
boy, then. –Scholar: The undertakers, sir.

THE troops, on their return from Windsor last week, got thoroughly
drenched. This is not to be wondered at, considering that the review was
held in the reign of Victoria.
