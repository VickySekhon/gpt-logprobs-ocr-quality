RAIDS ON CLUBS.

The police on Wednesday made two raids on supposed betting clubs in
London. The first was on the Falmouth club, 164, Strand. The premises
are situated on the first floor of a cigar shop, and extend through to
the back. At a given signal the three inspectors, who had 200 constables
in reserve, entered the cigar shop and proceeded along the passage until
their progress was blocked by a door. They knocked for admission, and
upon the door being opened rushed inside, completely overpowering the
man in charge, who was given at once into custody. The inspectors then
went to the first floor, and immediately their presence was discovered a
scene of great confusion ensued. The room was filled by some 20 or 30
men, who were crowding round a “tape machine” and a “ready-money
bookmaker," waiting the result of the second race of the day to come
through. A rush was made for the door by some of the men, but they found
that all escape had been cut off by that source, the staircase being
filled with policemen. The whole of the men found on the premises were
-arrested, including the proprietor. The greatest excitement prevailed
in the neighbourhood, each man, as he was taken out of the premises in
charge of two police officers being loudly cheered by the crowd which
had assembled. –Almost simultaneously with the raid on the Falmouth
club, and a short time before the City and Suburban handicap was being
run at Epsom the police effected an entrance to the Agar club, near
Buckingham-street, Strand. No opposition was made to the police, who,
under Inspector Robinson and Detective-serjeant Partridge, at once
arrested those found on the premises, to the number of about 40, and
they were conveyed in cabs to Bow-street police-station. By eight
o'clock on Wednesday evening all the prisoners had obtained bail.
