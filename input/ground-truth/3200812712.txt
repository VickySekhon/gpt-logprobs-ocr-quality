SHOCKING NEGLECT OF CHILDREN.

AT the Dartford Police-court, on Saturday, William and Harriet Roberts,
of Avery-street, Dartford, were summoned for neglecting their six
children, aged twelve, ten, eight, five, four, an eleven months, in a
manner likely to cause them unnecessary suffering. G. Patton, school
attendance officer, said that two or three months ago two of the
children were sent from school in consequence of their dirty condition,
and he visited them frequently at their homes. They were invariably
filthy and ragged. Miss Turner, assistant mistress at West-hill School,
said the children were sent home to get washed. She had complained of
their dirty appearance, and their clothing was insufficient. One of the
girls had twice called at her house begging for food. Inspector Hayes,
Royal Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Children, said that he
called at the defendants’ house on February 22nd. The woman told him
that she paid 4s. 6d. a week rent. The living-room was in a filthy
condition; the boards had not been washed for months. Two children sat
on the stools, their bare feet being in the ashes of the grate. They
only wore a cotton garment, tied round the neck, and open at the back.
All of them were in a terribly dirty condition. In the result the man
was fined £2 and costs, and the woman committed for two months’ hard
labour.
