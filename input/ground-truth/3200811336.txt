A FUNNY YOUNG MAN.

ROBERT SWINBURNE, described as of Alma-terrace, South Kensington,
gentleman, was brought up, on a warrant, for public examination, it
appearing that he had disregarded the process of the court, and had
failed to attend upon the official receiver for examination.

Mr. C. A. Pope attended as assistant receiver, and Mr. Marshall Hall on
behalf of creditors.

The bankrupt, in the course of his examination, said that he came of age
last year, and between January and July, 1892, he managed to get rid of
£5,000 all but a pound and a penny. He applied part of the money in
payment of old debts, including £1,060 in payment of a bill of £1,000
for which he had received £500. His items of expenditure included £20
per week for a tandem. "

Mr. Marshall Hall: Have you been attempting to borrow money on the
representation that you were Sir John Swinburne's eldest son?

The bankrupt said that he had not-only that he was the son of Sir John
Swinburne.

Mr. Marshall Hall produced a letter from the bankrupt containing the
expressions Love to Gipsy," and “Won't it be funny if I get in the
Divorce Court?" (A laugh.) He asked what he meant by such expressions.

The Bankrupt: Well, it would be funny, wouldn't it? (Laughter.)

Mr. M. Hall: How many women were you paying at one time?

The Bankrupt: Various.

Mr. M. Hall: Who was your mentor in showing you your way through the
world?

The Bankrupt: No one in particular. He further, stated that he had got
into trouble with the magistrates at Harrogate through getting "boozed,"
but it was a trifling matter.

The examination was adjourned, and the bankrupt was removed in custody.

A WIFE SHOT. —A deaf and dumb hawker, named Harris, was apprehended at
Leicester, on Friday, for shooting his wife in the head while in bed.
The parties. were married last Whitsuntide, and, after being separated,
agreed to live together on Thursday night. The woman was badly injured.

FEARFUL DISCOVERY. —An old woman, named Nuttall was on Thursday left at
home at Bacup, East Lancashire with her daughter's eight months old
child, while the rest of the family went to work. When the daughter:
returned she was horrified to discover her mother living dead on the
hearthstone with her head under the fire grate and the baby still
clasped in her arms. The baby miraculously escaped with only slight
burns. The woman. was burned about the shoulders.
