SHOCKING WIFE MURDER NEAR WOLVERHAMPTON.

--On Tuesday night Christopher Edwards, aged 35, a locksmith living at
Willenhall, near Wolverhampton, murdered his wife Rosannah, rather
younger than himself, under very revolting circumstances. Returning home
under the influence of drink, he ordered her to go upstairs and, after
looking the front and back doors, followed her with a heavy poker in one
hand and a candlestick in the other. In the upper room he struck her
across the eye, inflicting a fearful wound. The unfortunate woman called
for assistance, but to no purpose. Her husband then struck her a
succession of blows on the head with the knob end of the poker, and in a
few minutes she was dead. When arrested the prisoner said: --“She
commenced on me, and I punished her. I don’t think she’s dead. She
oughtn’t to be. I don’t think they’ll give more than 12 months, will
they?” He narrowly escaped lynching by the populace.

THE PRINCESS HENRY OF THE NETHERLANDS died at Luxemburg on Wednesday
morning.
