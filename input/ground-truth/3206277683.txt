GREAT BANK ROBBERY.

On Saturday some sensation was caused on its becoming known that a
London bank had been robbed of a large sum in bills. It appears the bank
is the London and Westminster bank, Lothbury. The bills had been
remitted from the various country banks, for which the London and
Westminster act as agents for the purpose of collecting in London, and
after being tied up in a parcel were deposited in a case in the country
manager's office. This apartment, situated on the left of the main
entrance, and at the end of a corridor, is easily accessible to
visitors, and it is supposed that while the officials and clerks were
busily engaged on Wednesday afternoon, the 16th inst., in the settlement
of stockbrokers accounts, someone entered the country manager's office
and abstracted the case of bills. They were all short bills, and their
value has been exaggerated.
