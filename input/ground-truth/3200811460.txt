CHARGE OF MURDER AT PLYMOUTH. —The Plymouth magistrates, on Monday,
committed a labourer, named Derrett, for trial on a charge of murdering
his wife by stabbing her to the heart on the 4th ult. They had been
married only four months. The man appeared to have been subjected to
considerable provocation from the deceased, with whom he formerly
lodged. Her former husband had been dead about twelve months, and her
grown-up daughters opposed the marriage.

THE SHOCKING CHARGE AGAINST AN INSPECTOR. —Lionel Hans Hamilton,
forty-five, lately Government Inspector of Factories for the
North-Eastern District, and Henry Dady, twenty-two, clerk, were charged,
at New castle, on Wednesday, jointly and separately, with indecency, and
with having conspired to induce others to commit shocking offences.
After some evidence had been taken the further hearing of the case was
adjourned for a week. Hamilton held a good position in society, and the
scandal has caused considerable sensation.

ARSON AT TOTTENHAM. —On Thursday morning, at twenty minutes to six, a
small coal shop in Stamford-road Tottenham, kept by a Mr. Ayres, was
found to be well. alight. The fire was ultimately extinguished, and the
damage was confined to the building. There have been four other
unsuccessful attempts to burn the place, principally directed against
the rear of the building, and in consequence the outbuildings have been
pulled down. A fortnight ago Mr. Ayres received an anonymous letter
threatening that since the writers could not burn him out at the back
they would "blow his brains out at the front."
